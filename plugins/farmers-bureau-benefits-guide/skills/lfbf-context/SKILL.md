# Louisiana Farm Bureau Federation (LFBF) — Contextual Reference

This skill provides accurate background knowledge for conversations about Louisiana Farm Bureau. Use it to inform responses without fabricating specific premium rates, enrollment procedures that may change, or local parish-specific details that cannot be verified.

## Organizational Structure

**Louisiana Farm Bureau Federation (LFBF):** The statewide organization, headquartered in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. A member of the American Farm Bureau Federation (AFBF) — the national organization representing farmers in all 50 states. LFBF is one of Louisiana's largest and most active advocacy organizations.

**Parish Farm Bureaus:** Louisiana has 64 parishes, and LFBF has a local Farm Bureau office in each. Membership is held at the parish level — a person joins their local parish Farm Bureau, not LFBF directly. The parish office is the member's primary point of contact for membership, insurance, and local services.

**LFBF Insurance:** The insurance affiliate of LFBF, operating as a separate legal entity but deeply integrated with LFBF membership. LFBF Insurance is one of Louisiana's largest property-casualty insurance companies, with agents co-located in parish Farm Bureau offices statewide. Joining LFBF does not automatically enroll a member in insurance — members separately purchase insurance products through LFBF Insurance agents.

**LFBF Foundation:** The philanthropic arm of LFBF, which funds Ag in the Classroom, member scholarships, and the disaster relief assistance program. Funded by donations from members and supporters.

## Membership Basics

- Open to ANY Louisiana resident — farmers and non-farmers alike
- Annual dues are set at the parish level and vary by parish; contact the local parish Farm Bureau or visit www.lfbf.org for current rates
- Most benefits activate upon membership and are available statewide; some programs vary by parish
- Members do not need to be farmers to access most membership benefits (insurance, discounts, prescription drug card, advocacy representation)

**Contact:**
- Website: www.lfbf.org
- Phone: 1-800-292-5330
- Local offices: All 64 Louisiana parishes

## Core Benefit Categories — Quick Reference

**1. Insurance (through LFBF Insurance, purchased separately from membership):**
- Auto insurance
- Homeowners and property insurance
- Farm and ranch insurance (structures, equipment, livestock, farm liability)
- Life insurance
- Supplemental health products (hospital indemnity, critical illness, accident)

**2. Financial Services:**
- LFBF Visa rewards credit card (no annual fee for members)
- Preferred access to Farm Credit / AgFirst Farm Credit lending (operating loans, real estate, equipment)
- Prescription drug discount card (free to members, accepted at most Louisiana pharmacies)

**3. Travel and Retail Discounts:**
- Hotel discounts: Best Western, Holiday Inn/IHG, Wyndham brand properties
- Car rental discounts: Enterprise, Hertz, National

**4. Agricultural Services (primarily for farmer members):**
- Commodity market price updates (weekly newsletters)
- Pest and disease alerts
- Farm management assistance through parish staff
- Soil testing coordination with LSU AgCenter

**5. Legal:**
- Agricultural attorney referral network (land disputes, easements, farm contracts, environmental compliance)

**6. Emergency:**
- LFBF Disaster Relief Program (grant assistance after major declared disasters — hurricane, flood, freeze — funded by LFBF Foundation, not insurance)

**7. Community and Education:**
- Ag in the Classroom (agricultural education program in Louisiana schools, especially urban/suburban)
- Young Farmers and Ranchers program (members aged 18–35, leadership development and competitions)
- Annual scholarships for Louisiana ag students

## Key Facts to Get Right

**Insurance vs. membership:** LFBF Insurance is a separate entity from LFBF membership. A member does not automatically receive insurance by joining — they purchase it separately. But LFBF agents in parish offices make this process seamless, and insurance is often the primary practical benefit that drives membership decisions.

**Disaster Relief vs. insurance:** The LFBF Disaster Relief Program provides grant assistance from Foundation funds, not insurance indemnity payments. It is activated for major declared disasters and supplements (not replaces) insurance coverage. Grant amounts and eligibility vary by disaster and available funding.

**Legal referral vs. legal representation:** LFBF provides a referral network to agricultural attorneys — not direct legal representation as a membership benefit. Members get help finding the right attorney; they pay for legal services separately.

**Farm input discounts:** These are negotiated partner programs, not a cooperative purchasing structure. Availability and specific savings vary by parish and current partner agreements. Members should confirm with their local parish office.

**Prescription drug card:** This is a discount card (like GoodRx), not insurance. Savings vary by medication, pharmacy, and current pricing. The card is free to members and can reduce costs significantly on generic medications.
